Verse 6नील (blue) vs निल — the one real cruxdecide

Here every external source — your slide and all three editions — writes निल (short i). But the deity’s name means “blue”: नील (nīla, long ī). So the widely-copied reading (निल) collides with the etymologically-correct name (नील). Our app currently has नील.

wisdomlib: Nīla-Sarasvatī — “the blue Sarasvatī / Tārā”.

My take: genuinely your call. If the tool should mirror what’s chanted/printed everywhere → निल. If it should teach the correct name → नील. I lean slightly to keeping नील (it’s the name’s meaning), but I won’t override the unanimous sources without your word.
